Ward 2 has the greatest total square footage of dog parks in Washington, D.C., as seen in the chart, with Ward 3 closely behind. These two stick out as pretty clearly having the most square footage, and Wards 7 and 8 stick out as clearly having the least. This chart is based on data supplied by the D.C. government and publicized on Open Data D.C. It was compiled by adding up the square footage of all of the parks in each ward. Ward 8 has the second largest individual park, but because it only has one, it still lags behind most other wards. Meanwhile, three of Ward 3’s four parks are among the five smallest parks. They have the most parks but not the greatest total area because Ward 2 has the biggest park. Still, the difference between those two parks is basically negligible and the point still stands that Wards 7 and 8 are lacking in space safe for dogs. Wards 7 and 8 also have higher populations than Ward 3 which creates a higher demand for less space. Having a greater number of smaller parks like Ward 3 has is probably the preferable set up, too. The whole issue with Ward 7 previously having no dog parks was that residents, and their dogs, would have to travel to other parts of the city or Maryland or Virginia. Ward 3’s are more spread out which gives people more options closer to where they live. The whole section of D.C. east of the Anacostia River and south of Fort Dupont has nowhere to safely and legally take their dogs off leash. It’s a dog park desert, and the only other comparable area in the city is around Catholic University but the underserved portion of southeast D.C. is bigger and more removed from options.
