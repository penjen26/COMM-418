Sports fans are incredibly superstitious. I'd like to track team performance in home jerseys, away jerseys and alternate jerseys. Let's do this for the entire NHL. This data is possible to collect as teams publicize which jerseys they wear and records are public. This is timely to now, May 2026, because the playoffs are going on and teams have to commit to one set of jerseys. The Pittsburgh Penguins, for example, chose to wear alternate yellow jerseys at home in the playoffs instead of their typical black jerseys. This is different than just home and away splits because home jerseys and alternates would split time for home games, and sometimes when one team has white alternate jerseys (liike the LA Kings used to) then the away team has to wear their home jerseys anyways.
My specific question is whether teams perform better or worse than their overall winning percentage when wearing alternate jerseys. I don't think there's anything major to claim from this besides that people are superstitious and these questions are fun to answer. Does "look good, feel good" apply to pro sports in a measurable way?
I think the easiest way to do this is to manually go through a team's record and check which jerseys they were wearing and just do this for all 82 games. You could probably knock out both teams in any particular game at once, so hopefully I wouldn't actually have to look at all 82 games for all 32 teams.
