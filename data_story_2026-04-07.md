Fun data story from the New York Times Upshot! They had reporters take typing tests to measure their words per minute. Felt oddly timely for me because I did a typing test for a friend's psych lab last week (I got 69 WPM). There are a few references to specific reporters' speeds and the overall range, but I would have loved to see a chart. It would be interesting to see if there is correlation between age and speed, as the article somewhat implies, so I'd like to see a scatterplot. If that doesn't actually look good for the sake of the article, I'd maybe do a box and whisker plot or maybe a version of that more clearly understandable to the general public. The base information is fun but I was expecting more from the Upshot. They usually go further with these ideas.
